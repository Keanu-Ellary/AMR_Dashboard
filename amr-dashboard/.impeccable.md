# Design Context

## Users & Purpose
Researchers monitoring water quality in the Apies River and surrounding areas. The tool must provide clinical precision and professional clarity, allowing researchers to quickly identify trends, anomalies, and risk zones (AMR/Algae).

## Brand Personality
**Scientific, Professional, Minimalist.**
The interface should feel like a high-end research tool—uncluttered, objective, and authoritative.

## Aesthetic Direction
**Scientific Minimalist (Light Mode).**
- **Palette**: Clean whites, subtle tinted neutrals (e.g., cool grays or very light blues), and high-contrast typography.
- **Accents**: Functional use of color (Red/Yellow/Green for risk zones) without decorative gradients or "glow" effects.
- **Typography**: Refined, legible sans-serif for data; crisp, distinct headers.
- **Space**: Generous whitespace to reduce cognitive load; purposeful groupings rather than "card-in-card" nesting.

## Design Principles
1. **Data First**: UI elements should recede; data (maps, charts, tables) should be the hero.
2. **Functional Color**: Color must convey meaning (risk levels, status), never decoration.
3. **Professional Restraint**: No generic "AI slop" (no neon, no heavy rounded borders, no bounce animations).
4. **Structural Clarity**: Use clear hierarchy and consistent alignment to guide the researcher's eye.
